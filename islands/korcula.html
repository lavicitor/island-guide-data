Getting There

Multiple connections from Split (via catamaran to Korčula town, or car ferry to Vela Luka on the western tip). A short passenger ferry runs from Orebić on the Pelješac peninsula directly to Korčula town — useful if driving from Dubrovnik.

💬 Local Knowledge

Curated tips — not scraped

⚠

Korčula town gets very busy with cruise ship day-trippers in July and August — mornings are crowded, evenings are peaceful once the ships leave.

⚠

Vela Luka (the car ferry port) is a separate town from Korčula town — factor in the transfer time when planning connections.

✦

Pošip and Grk wine

Korčula produces two indigenous white wines — Pošip and Grk — that are genuinely excellent and rarely found outside Croatia. Ask for them by name in any konoba.

✦

Moreška sword dance

A traditional sword dance performed only in Korčula town, with roots going back centuries. Performed regularly in summer for tourists but it is an authentic local tradition, not a fabricated one.
