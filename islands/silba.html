The journey from Zadar takes approximately 1.5–2 hours depending on the route. Friday evening ferries in July and August fill quickly — arrive at the port at least 45 minutes early. The Gaženica route (out of Zadar's newer port) is the more reliable year-round service. Book tickets in advance during peak season at jadrolinija.hr.

  • •No cars are permitted on the island — leave yours in Zadar or use the paid car park near Gaženica port.
  • •Bikes can be brought on the Gaženica ferry. The island is small enough to walk everywhere.
  • •Return ferry times in peak season sell out — book the return leg before you arrive.

Practical

Euronet

Bring cash from Zadar. There is one Euronet ATM near the ferry terminal but it can run out in peak season. Konoba Mul is confirmed cash only. Other restaurants increasingly accept cards but do not rely on it.

Medical

Seasonal medical office operates in the village centre during summer months (roughly June–September). Outside season, the nearest hospital is in Zadar.

Accommodation (7 options)

Recommended stay: 2 nights. Silba works as a day trip from Zadar but you will spend most of your time on ferries. Two nights is the minimum to feel the pace of the island properly.

Internet

Limited. MIK café has Wi-Fi. Mobile signal (HT/T-Com) is usable in the village but weak on the southern beaches.

Shopping

One small shop for basics. Stock up on supplies in Zadar before coming — prices on the island are higher and selection limited.

Freedom from Serfdom Plaque

A plaque marking the day in 1852 when Silba's residents bought their freedom from feudal rule.

💬 Easy to walk past — look for it near the central square.

💬 Local tips

  • •The inscription reads: 'On 19 March 1852, our ancestors bought and freed the island from serfdom'.
  • •Small but historically significant — the islanders collectively purchased their own freedom, which was unusual for the period.

The island's most recognisable landmark — a round stone tower with views over the whole island.

💬 Local Knowledge

Curated tips — not scraped

⚠

No cars on the island — and no bikes during summer months either. Everything is on foot.

⚠

One ATM. Bring more cash than you think you need.

⚠

Mobile internet is limited. Download maps offline before you arrive.

⚠

The island is very small and very social — the same faces appear everywhere. Respect the community.

⚠

Last ferry back to Zadar can be early evening in low season. Check the return schedule before you come.

✦

Evening korzo

Like most Croatian coastal communities, Silba has an unofficial evening promenade. The stretch between the two harbours comes alive around 8–9pm. Worth experiencing even if you're not eating out.

✦

South of the island

Most tourists stay near the village. The southern tip of the island (30-minute walk) has more isolated beaches and a noticeably different, wilder feel. Worth the walk on a calm day.

✦

The silence

No cars means no background engine noise — something you only notice once it's gone. At night the island is genuinely quiet in a way that almost nowhere in Croatia is any more.
